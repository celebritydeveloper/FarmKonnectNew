Aquaculture Project

The earliest of our projects is the aquaculture, starting from a single pond in Mokola, we now have farms for production of up to 100 Tons of catfish per month. Our operation is not limited to catfish, but after 7 years of research and deliberate investments, we are now at the fore front of production of Pangasius hypothalmus, which is a more palatable and healthier fresh/brackish water fish. The acceptance of Pangaius Niloticus promises a better future than catfish. Not limiting our operations, we also delve into other species such as Gymnachos Niloticus, Heterotis, Tilapia, and many other fresh water species that could be farmed in Nigeria. With a population of 190 Million, fish deficit in excess of 2 Million Tons per annum and the profitability of aquaculture is unlimited. It is the major source of animal protein to African countries and can be consumed both in local and export market. The vision of FarmKonnect is to be the biggest producers of these fishes in Nigeria, then in West Africa.

Igbeja Snail Village

Working in partnership with and under the tutelage of Touchstone Snails Technology Limited, Lanarca, Cyprus, we are currently developing Africa’s largest snail facility tagged Igbeja Snail Village. With a setup estimate of 13.7 Million Dollars (5 Billion Naira) the first phase of the project will house 100 Units of snail breeding facility. We believe that the project should not just be about farming, we should also add glamour to our work, so we will put a touch of tourism and world class hospitality. It is opened to investors as per phase, the first phase is the farm construction and operation which will produce minimum of 2.6 Million Kg (26,000 Metric Tons) of snails per annum, with a return on investment as high as 59% from the first year of production. All snails produced are for the export market. There is also a future consideration towards local processing for slime, meat and caviar.

FarmKonnect Portal

In order to enhance the productivity of African farmers, we developed a portal with mobile app access. This app is designed not for profit, however a token is charged from user to provide a lifetime support. The app will be for investments management, farm management, and directory of farmers, link to market and consultancy services, as well as agricultural library and other services that will enable a farmer easily manage and sell his farm activities and produce. The portal is also available on Android and iOS mobile applications.

Ostrich Farming

We have carried out series of research on Ostrich farming and we know it is a huge under tapped opportunity in Nigeria. A great source of lean red meat with a relatively better Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) than cattle; therefore, promising great profits. Due to the prolific nature of the flightless bird and its excellent feed conversion rate, it is a rewarding venture that requires large space and capital to thrive. After having tested this venture, FarmKonnect is working toward raising a 10,000 mature female ostrich farm.

Insect Farming

Malnutrition is a dimension to look at in Africa’s battle for food insecurity. Poverty has made access to animal protein difficult to many. Apart from this, the global community is fast looking at the other dimensions of sourcing healthier and affordable high quality protein and probiotics. Insects promise to offer such for the future. It is high time we began to look towards raring these beautiful creatures for their benefits. At FarmKonnect, our focus of research and investment include but not limited to locust, cricket, edible worms, honey and bumble bees, cockroaches.

Project Approach

Since our projects are majorly financed by sponsors, we need to put adequate effort towards ensuring the success of the projects; consequently, we have four stages of our projects:

Testing

Before considering any project for funding, we first test the project on very small scale to be sure that it can be supported locally and possibly result in profit. If we find a project feasible, we design a large scale concept around it.

Pilot

Before going full blown, we start off all large scale projects with a pilot phase which is usually 10 to 20 percent of the full scale. This allows us to mitigate risk and be assured of the smooth running of our projects when fully commenced.

Intermediate Scale

At the intermediate scale, we proceed from 10/20 percent towards 50/60 per cent scale of the entire project, monitoring the metrics to be sure that the project remains feasible at the full scale.

Large Scale

At this stage, we go fully out on our projects, which may transcend beyond production into processing and distribution.

Please note that regardless of the scale at any moment, we are highly focussed on risk management and profitability of the projects.
